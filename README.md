# **Customer Churn Analysis using SQL**

## **Project Overview**

- Customer churn refers to customers who stop using a company’s services.
- Understanding churn helps businesses improve **customer retention** and reduce revenue loss.
- In this project, **SQL** was used to analyze a telecom customer dataset and identify patterns that influence customer churn.

---

## **Problem Statement**

- The goal of this project is to analyze customer data and identify the key factors that lead to **customer churn**.
- These insights can help businesses take **proactive steps to retain customers**.

---

## **Dataset Description**

- The dataset contains customer information from a telecom company.
- It includes demographic details, account information, and service usage.

**Dataset Details**

- **Records:** ~7000+
- **Columns:** 20+

**Important Features**

- **CustomerID** – Unique customer identifier  
- **Gender** – Customer gender  
- **Tenure** – Number of months the customer stayed with the company  
- **Contract Type** – Type of contract (Monthly / Yearly)  
- **MonthlyCharges** – Monthly bill amount  
- **TotalCharges** – Total amount paid by the customer  
- **PaymentMethod** – Payment method used by the customer  
- **InternetService** – Type of internet service  
- **Churn** – Indicates whether the customer left the service (Yes/No)

---

## **Tools Used**

- **SQL**

- **Excel**

---

## **SQL Concepts Used**

- **SELECT**
- **WHERE**
- **GROUP BY**
- **HAVING**
- **Aggregate Functions (COUNT, AVG, SUM)**
- **CASE Statements**

---

## **Analysis Performed**

- Calculated the **overall churn rate**
- Analyzed churn based on **contract type**
- Compared churn across **payment methods**
- Examined the relationship between **monthly charges and churn**
- Studied the impact of **customer tenure on churn**

---

## **Key Findings**

- Customers with **month-to-month contracts** have the highest churn rate.
- Customers with **higher monthly charges** tend to churn more frequently.
- Customers with **longer tenure** are less likely to leave the service.

---

## **Conclusion**

- This project demonstrates how **SQL can be used to analyze customer data** and uncover meaningful insights.
- These insights can help businesses **improve customer retention strategies** and reduce churn.
