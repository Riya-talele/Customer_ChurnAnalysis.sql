# Customer Churn Analysis using SQL

## Project Overview

Customer churn is a critical business metric that measures the percentage of customers who stop using a company’s services over a given period. High churn not only impacts revenue but also increases customer acquisition costs, making retention a key focus area for businesses.

In this project, SQL was used to analyze a dataset of 5,000+ customers to understand patterns and behaviors associated with churn. The analysis focuses on identifying trends across customer demographics, subscription types, and usage behavior.

The goal is to transform raw customer data into actionable insights that can help businesses:

-Improve customer retention
-Identify high risk customer segments
-Optimize pricing and service strategies
-Enhance overall customer experience

---

## Problem Statement

The objective of this project is to analyze customer data and identify the key factors that lead to customer churn.

These insights can help businesses take proactive steps to retain customers, reduce churn, and improve overall customer satisfaction.

---

## Dataset Description

The dataset contains customer level information including demographics, account details, and service usage behavior.

## Dataset Summary

Total Records: 5,000+
Total Features: 20+

## Important Features

CustomerID – Unique identifier for each customer
Gender – Customer demographic information
Tenure – Number of months the customer stayed with the company
Contract Type – Monthly or yearly subscription
MonthlyCharges – Monthly billing amount
TotalCharges – Total amount paid by the customer
PaymentMethod – Mode of payment used
InternetService – Type of service used
Churn – Indicates whether the customer left the service (Yes or No)

## Data Preparation

The dataset was cleaned by handling missing values and fixing inconsistencies. Data quality was improved to ensure accurate analysis. Basic transformations were performed to make the data more useful for analysis.

---

## Analysis Performed

Calculated overall customer churn rate
Analyzed churn based on contract type
Compared churn across different payment methods
Examined the relationship between monthly charges and churn
Studied how customer tenure impacts churn behavior

---

## Key Insights

Customers on month-to-month contracts have a significantly higher churn rate compared to long term customers.
Customers with higher monthly charges are more likely to churn.
Customers with shorter tenure tend to leave earlier, indicating weak early engagement.
Certain customer groups based on payment behavior and service usage show higher churn risk.

---

## Business Impact and Recommendations

Encourage customers to switch to long-term contracts to improve retention
Focus on early engagement strategies for new customers
Review pricing strategies for customers with higher monthly charges
Target high-risk customers with personalized retention strategies
